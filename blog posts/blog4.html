Education

Brought Bronze Home

For the past years of studying hard at Olivarez College Tagaytay, I finally earned my first achievement, being an awardee.



People say that hard work pays off, I find it hard to believe after all the work I put into my time in college. Studying really hard just to pass the exams, being overly responsible to every activities. It has come to the point where I am already overthinking it everytime, yet where's my reward for my excellence?


The harder you work for something, the greater you'll feel when you achieve it.
-- Some lies

We'll never really know when we will achieve something we've been working hard for, especially when it's been so long. I took college seriously yet I was never awarded for it. But just this month of November, I achieved something I never thought I would achieve.



The day I became an awardee

I felt lucky to have friends that also takes this little studying thingy seriously. Nobody wants to waste the ginormous tuition fee they paid for. The results, I, along with my friends, became awardees in recognition of our academic excellence at Olivarez College Tagaytay.

This blog will serve as my cute little gallery about that day.



  • 3 Idiots

    • The thesis boys of TypeTrials, expected to be roasted by the defense day.

    Very surprising this trio got an award, one is a Dean's Lister. Crazy, isn't it?

  • Called on stage

    • Who would've thought I'd be on the screen showing my excellence? That's so new, very funny cause the last time was a long time ago.

    The best part about this celebration is when my very own biological mother believed, my mom has gently, proudly put the medal on my shoulders. Such a rare moment but hopefully it'll happen again soon.

  • This is us

    • Celebrating recognition together with my friends is something I would treasure for the rest of my life.

    Pick the right people, try to succeed together, even just for once. It'll be unforgettable.

  • The olympic bite

    • I had to do it, having a medal is so rare bro!

    Conclusion

    This award was only based on one semester, meaning it didn't include my performances on other years of my studying. That'll be left buried in the sands. Even though it is quite disappointing, I chose to be happy to save this day for good. It only happens once, it may never happen again. So I celebrated while I can, and it was worth it. I'll just do better next time, and maybe I can celebrate again.
